Singer, composer, actor and entrepreneur, Carlos Vives is one of the most emblematic artists of Colombia and one of the most important figures of Latin music. With more than 20 million albums sold around the world, he is considered a pioneer of the new Colombian and Latin American sound. He was the first Colombian artist to win a Grammy Award and, to date, has taken home 2 Grammys and 11 Latin Grammys. Some of his great hymns are: The cold drop, Let me in, Fresh fruit, Pa 'Mayté, I was born again, The land of oblivion, Dancing with you and success The bicycle, which shares with Shakira. 2017 was a year full of success for Carlos Vives. It reached more than 18 countries and 78 shows around the world, more than 962 hours live, selling approximately more than half a million tickets. Their hits are heard on digital music platforms by more than 15 million monthly listeners.

Carlos Vives, the man who modernized one of the best artistic expressions in Colombia, was predestined to creation and, especially, to music. And, through his art, and perhaps unknowingly, also predestined to become the representative of that pure and honest Colombian, who with each act praises his country. Vives always had the artistic virus to the surface. In 1982, with a little more than 20 years, he began his career as an actor in the children's television program Pequeños gigantes. In the three long years that he was on the screen he showed that, although small, he left giant footprints in his path. In 1984 he graduated as a publicist from the Jorge Tadeo Lozano University, with a postgraduate degree in television production. He starred, along with great actors, various series and soap operas including El Pharaoh and Tuyo is my heart, being remembered for embodying famous characters like "Gallito" Ramírez and the famous composer Rafael Escalona. This samara captivated crowds in America and Europe with the rhythms of Classics of La Provincia, album with which he broke his own sales record (Triple Gold Record and Triple Platinum Record in 1993 and 1995), which boasted the soundtrack of Escalona. In the same way, he managed to locate in the first places of the pop listings songs of juglares vallenatos like Emiliano Zuleta, Juancho Polo Valencia, Luis Enrique Martinez and Carlos Huertas, among others.

Deep Heart and More Deep Heart, they generated six # 1 in Billboard's Hot Latin Songs, with hits like: When we meet again Marc Anthony, Dance with you, The sea of your eyes and I was reborn, which became a hymn in Latin America, dominating radios in Mexico, Venezuela, Chile, Argentina, Ecuador, Peru, Central America and Colombia. The Más corazón profunda tour became the most successful tour in the history of Colombia, with more than 200,000 fans filling stadiums in five concerts.
